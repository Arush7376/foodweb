Corn Fried Rice

Corn fried rice is a tasty and colorful variation of traditional fried rice. It incorporates sweet corn kernels into the dish, adding a natural sweetness and a delightful texture. Here's a basic recipe for making corn fried rice:

Ingredients:

  1. 2 cups cooked jasmine rice (preferably cooled or chilled)
  2. 1 cup sweet corn kernels (fresh or frozen)
  3. 1 cup mixed vegetables (carrots, peas, bell peppers, and/or corn)
  4. 1/2 cup finely chopped onions
  5. 2 cloves garlic, minced
  6. 2 eggs, beaten
  7. 3 tablespoons soy sauce
  8. 1 tablespoon oyster sauce (optional)
  9. 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  10. 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  11. Salt and pepper to taste
  12. Green onions, chopped, for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Prep Ingredients:
  • If you haven't done so already, cook the jasmine rice and let it cool. It's ideal to use rice that has been refrigerated for a few hours or overnight, as it tends to fry better.
  1. Cook Eggs:
  • In the same wok, add a bit more oil if needed. Saut√© the chopped onions and minced garlic until fragrant.
  • Add the mixed vegetables and sweet corn. Stir-fry until the vegetables are tender yet still crisp.
  1. Add Rice:
  • Add the cooked and cooled jasmine rice to the wok. Break up any clumps and stir-fry the rice with the vegetables.
  1. Season the Rice:
  • Pour soy sauce and oyster sauce over the rice and vegetables. Add sesame oil for flavor. Stir well to ensure even distribution of the sauces.
  1. Combine with Eggs:
  • Once the rice is well-coated with the sauces, add the scrambled eggs back into the wok. Mix everything together until the eggs are evenly distributed throughout the rice.
  1. Season to Taste:
  • Taste the fried rice and adjust the seasoning with salt and pepper as needed. You can also add more soy sauce if desired.
  1. Garnish and Serve:
  • Garnish the corn fried rice with chopped green onions if you like. Serve hot and enjoy!

Feel free to customize this recipe by adding other ingredients such as diced chicken, shrimp, or tofu for additional protein. The key to a good fried rice is using cold, cooked rice and ensuring that the wok or skillet is hot enough for a quick and even stir-fry.
