Gobi Manchurian

Gobi Manchurian is a popular Indo-Chinese dish made with cauliflower florets that are battered, fried until crispy, and then tossed in a flavorful Manchurian sauce. It's a delicious and tangy appetizer that's perfect for those who enjoy a bit of spice. Here's a basic recipe for Gobi Manchurian:

Ingredients:

For the Cauliflower Fritters:

  1. 1 medium-sized cauliflower, cut into florets
  2. 1 cup all-purpose flour (maida)
  3. 1/2 cup cornstarch
  4. 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  5. 1 teaspoon soy sauce
  6. 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  7. Salt to taste
  8. Water (as needed to make a thick batter)
  9. Oil for deep frying
  10. For the Manchurian Sauce:

For the Manchurian Sauce:

    1. 1 tablespoon oil
    2. 1 tablespoon finely chopped garlic
    3. 1 tablespoon finely chopped ginger
    4. 1/2 cup finely chopped onions
    5. 1/2 cup finely chopped bell peppers (capsicum)
    6. 2 tablespoons soy sauce
    7. 1 tablespoon chili sauce
    8. 1 tablespoon ketchup
    9. 1 tablespoon vinegar
    10. 1 tablespoon cornstarch mixed with 3 tablespoons
      11. water (for thickening)
    11. 1/2 teaspoon sugar
    12. Salt and pepper to taste
    13. Chopped green onions for garnish

    Instructions:

    For the Cauliflower Fritters:

    1. In a large mixing bowl, combine all-purpose flour, cornstarch, ginger-garlic paste, soy sauce, red chili powder, and salt.
    2. Add water gradually to form a thick batter. It should be thick enough to coat the cauliflower florets.
    3. Dip each cauliflower floret into the batter, ensuring it's well-coated.
    4. Heat oil in a deep pan for frying. Fry the cauliflower florets until they turn golden brown and crispy. Remove them and place them on paper towels to absorb excess oil.

    For the Manchurian Sauce:

    1. In a separate pan, heat oil over medium heat. Add chopped garlic and ginger, saut√© for a minute until fragrant.
    2. Add chopped onions and bell peppers. Stir-fry until they become tender yet still slightly crisp.
    3. Add soy sauce, chili sauce, ketchup, vinegar, sugar, salt, and pepper. Mix well.
    4. In a small bowl, make a slurry by mixing cornstarch with water. Add this slurry to the pan to thicken the sauce. Stir continuously to avoid lumps.
    5. Once the sauce thickens, add the fried cauliflower florets to the pan. Toss the cauliflower in the sauce until they are well-coated.

    7. Serve Gobi Manchurian hot as an appetizer or with steamed rice/noodles as a main dish.

    Enjoy your homemade Gobi Manchurian! Adjust the spice levels and sweetness according to your taste.
