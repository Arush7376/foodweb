Chilli Gobi

Chilli Gobi is a popular Indo-Chinese dish that features crispy cauliflower florets tossed in a flavorful and spicy sauce. Here's a general recipe for Chilli Gobi:

Ingredients:

For Batter:

  1. 1 cup cauliflower florets
  2. 1/4 cup finely chopped spring onions
  3. 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  4. 1/4 cup cornflour
  5. 1 tsp ginger-garlic paste
  6. 1 tsp soy sauce
  7. 1/2 tsp black pepper powder
  8. Salt to taste
  9. Oil for deep frying

Steps:

For Sauce:

  1. 2 tbsp oil
  2. 1 tbsp finely chopped garlic
  3. 1 tbsp finely chopped ginger
  4. 1/2 cup finely chopped spring onions
  5. 1/4 cup finely chopped capsicum (bell pepper)
  6. 2 tbsp soy sauce
  7. 1 tbsp chili sauce
  8. 1 tsp vinegar
  9. 1 tsp sugar
  10. Salt to taste
  11. 1 cup water
  12. 1.5 tbsp cornflour mixed with 3 tbsp water (to make a slurry)

instructions:

For Batter:

  1. In a bowl, mix together the finely chopped vegetables, spring onions, all-purpose flour, cornflour, ginger-garlic paste, soy sauce, black pepper powder, and salt.
  2. Dip cauliflower florets into the batter, ensuring they are well coated
  3. Heat oil in a pan for deep frying. Once the oil is hot, fry the vegetable balls until they turn golden brown. Remove and set aside.

For Sauce:

  1. In a separate pan, heat 2 tbsp of oil.
  2. Add chopped garlic and ginger. Sauté for a minute until they become aromatic.
  3. Add chopped spring onions and capsicum. Cook for a couple of minutes until they are slightly tender.
  4. Once the sauce reaches the desired consistency, add the fried vegetable balls. Toss them in the sauce until well coated.
  5. Garnish with chopped spring onions.

Chilli Gobi is a delectable Indo-Chinese dish featuring crispy cauliflower florets coated in a flavorful and spicy sauce. The dish begins by battering cauliflower with a mixture of all-purpose flour, cornflour, and spices before deep-frying until golden and crisp. The sauce, a blend of garlic, ginger, onions, and bell peppers stir-fried with soy sauce, chili sauce, and other seasonings, is then combined with the fried cauliflower. The result is a savory and spicy dish, often garnished with chopped spring onions, making it a popular appetizer or side dish in Indian and Chinese cuisine
