Spring Rolls

Sure, here's a basic recipe for making vegetable spring rolls, a popular and delicious Asian appetizer. Spring rolls are typically filled with a combination of fresh vegetables, sometimes meat or shrimp, and wrapped in thin sheets of rice paper. They are often served with a dipping sauce. This recipe is for vegetarian spring rolls:

Ingredients:

For the Filling:

  1. Rice vermicelli noodles (cooked according to package instructions)
  2. Rice paper wrappers (spring roll wrappers)
  3. 1 cup shredded cabbage
  4. 1 cup julienned carrots
  5. 1 cup thinly sliced bell peppers (red, yellow, or orange)
  6. 1 cup bean sprouts
  7. 2 cup chopped fresh cilantro
  8. 1/4 cup chopped fresh mint leaves
  9. 1/4 cup chopped green onions
  10. Optional: Cooked and shredded tofu, shrimp, or chicken

For the Dipping Sauce:

  1. 1/4 cup soy sauce
  2. 2 tablespoons hoisin sauce
  3. 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  4. 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  5. 1 teaspoon sugar
  6. 1 clove garlic, minced
  7. Optional: Sriracha or chili garlic sauce for heat

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Filling:
  • Cook the rice vermicelli noodles according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  • Prep all the vegetables and herbsâ€”shred the cabbage, julienne the carrots, slice the bell peppers, and chop the cilantro, mint, and green onions.
  1. Softening Rice Paper Wrappers:
  • Fill a large shallow dish with warm water.
  • Dip one rice paper wrapper into the warm water for about 10-15 seconds until it becomes pliable but still slightly firm. Place it on a clean, damp kitchen towel.
  1. Assemble the Spring Rolls:
  • Place a small portion of the cooked rice vermicelli noodles on the lower third of the rice paper wrapper.
  • Add a handful of shredded cabbage, julienned carrots, sliced bell peppers, bean sprouts, and chopped herbs.
  • If you're using tofu, shrimp, or chicken, add a small amount to the filling.
  1. Rolling the Spring Rolls:
  • Fold the sides of the rice paper over the filling, then fold the bottom of the wrapper over the filling.
  • Roll the spring roll tightly from the bottom to the top, sealing the edge with a little water if needed.
  1. Repeat:
  • Repeat the process until all the filling is used.
  1. Prepare the Dipping Sauce:
  • In a small bowl, whisk together soy sauce, hoisin sauce, rice vinegar, sesame oil, sugar, and minced garlic. Add sriracha or chili garlic sauce if you like it spicy.
  1. Serve:
  • Serve the spring rolls whole or cut in half, with the dipping sauce on the side.

Enjoy your homemade vegetable spring rolls! Feel free to customize the filling according to your preferences.
