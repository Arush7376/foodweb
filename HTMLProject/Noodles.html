Manchurian is a popular Indo-Chinese dish that consists of deep-fried vegetables or meatballs tossed in a flavorful, tangy, and slightly sweet sauce. There are variations of Manchurian, such as vegetable Manchurian, chicken Manchurian, and gobi (cauliflower) Manchurian. Here's a general recipe for vegetable Manchurian:

Ingredients:

For Noodles

  1. 200g noodles
  2. 1 cup sliced bell peppers
  3. 1 cup julienned carrots
  4. 1 cup sliced cabbage
  5. 2-3 cloves of garlic, minced
  6. 2 tbsp soy sauce
  7. 1 tbsp chili sauce
  8. 1 tsp vinegar
  9. 1 tsp sugar
  10. Salt to taste
  11. 1 cup water

instructions:

For Noodles

  1. Cook the noodles according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  2. Heat oil in a wok or a large pan over medium-high heat.
  3. Add the minced garlic and saut√© for a minute until it becomes fragrant.
  4. Toss in the sliced bell peppers, julienned carrots, and sliced cabbage. Stir-fry for 2-3 minutes until the vegetables are slightly tender but still crisp. Add the cooked noodles to the wok along with soy sauce. Toss everything together until the noodles and vegetables are well combined.
  5. Season with salt, pepper, and chili sauce (if using). Stir well.
  6. Cook for an additional 2-3 minutes, ensuring the noodles are heated through and well-coated with the sauce.
