Burnt Fried Rice

Burnt Garlic Fried Rice is a flavorful and aromatic dish that gets its distinct taste from the slightly burnt or roasted garlic. Here's a simple recipe for Burnt Garlic Fried Rice:

Ingredients:

  1. 2 cups cooked and cooled rice (preferably day-old rice)
  2. 6-8 cloves garlic, finely chopped
  3. 1 cup mixed vegetables (carrots, peas, corn, bell peppers, etc.), finely chopped
  4. 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  5. 1 tablespoon oyster sauce (optional)
  6. 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  7. 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  8. Salt and pepper to taste
  9. Green onions (scallions), chopped for garnish
  10. Sesame seeds for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

  1. If using freshly cooked rice, spread it on a tray to cool or use day-old rice. Cold rice works best for fried rice as it is less sticky
  2. Heat vegetable oil in a wok or a large pan over medium heat.
  3. Add finely chopped garlic and saut√© until it starts to turn golden brown. Be careful not to burn it completely; you want a slightly burnt and aromatic flavor
  4. Add the mixed vegetables to the pan and stir-fry for 2-3 minutes until they are tender yet still crisp.
  5. Add the cooled rice to the pan. Use a spatula to break up any clumps and mix the rice with the vegetables and garlic.
  6. Pour soy sauce and oyster sauce (if using) over the rice. Stir well to ensure that the sauces are evenly distributed.
  7. Drizzle sesame oil over the rice for added flavor. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  8. Continue to stir-fry the rice for an additional 3-5 minutes, allowing it to heat through and absorb the flavors.
  9. Garnish with chopped green onions and sesame seeds for added freshness and crunch.
  10. Transfer the Burnt Garlic Fried Rice to a serving dish and serve hot

Enjoy your flavorful Burnt Garlic Fried Rice as a delicious side dish or as a meal on its own! Feel free to customize the recipe by adding protein such as tofu, chicken, shrimp, or any other ingredients you like.
